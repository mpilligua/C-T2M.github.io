Abstract

TODO: one-paragraph abstract. C-T2M generates human motion from a text caption while following user-specified XZ path constraints. We decouple the problem into a small closed-loop trajectory controller, a caption-driven body GPT, and a deterministic recompose step that drops the body's pose onto the controller's path.

Introduction

TODO: motivate the gap. Text-to-motion models like T2M-GPT produce realistic motion from a caption, but the user has no control over the resulting root path. Adding path constraints as extra caption tokens or conditioning signals broke gait in our experiments. We propose a decoupled pipeline that keeps the body generator intact and adds path control as a separate, lightweight module.

Method

TODO: describe the three blocks. (i) Trajectory controller, a closed-loop GRU that takes (caption, waypoints) and rolls out a clean root path P[T,2] plus integrated heading and yaw offset. (ii) Body GPT, a finetuned T2M-GPT trained on walk and jog clips with sliding-window sampling; its own root translation is discarded. (iii) Recompose, a deterministic step that places the body's pose on the controller's path, time-warps so the feet do not slide, and rotates the body to face head + face_sign·delta.

Key capabilities

Caption-conditioned

The body GPT generates motion that matches the input text, inherited from T2M-GPT.

Constraint-following

A small trajectory controller follows user-specified XZ waypoints; final waypoint error 0.013–0.027 m.

Long sustained motion

Sliding-window training and inference yield ~26 s of coherent motion at 30 fps, past the single-pass cap.

Interactive demo

A viser web app lets users click waypoints, pick from preset trajectories, or run the body alone.
